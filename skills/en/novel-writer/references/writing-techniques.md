## Narrative Perspective

### First Person (I)
- **Pros**: Strong intimacy, deep immersion
- **Cons**: Limited information, highly subjective
- **Best for**: Psychological novels, mysteries

### Third Person Limited (He/She)
- **Pros**: Flexible, can access character's inner world
- **Cons**: Limited perspective
- **Best for**: Most novels

### Third Person Omniscient
- **Pros**: Comprehensive information, free to switch
- **Cons**: Creates distance
- **Best for**: Epic works

### Multiple Perspectives
- Multiple characters' perspectives alternate
- Each perspective has a unique voice
- Shows the story from different angles

## Scene Building

### Scene Elements
- **Time**: When does it happen?
- **Location**: Where does it happen?
- **Characters**: Who is present?
- **Conflict**: What happens?
- **Change**: What's different by the end?

### Scene Structure
1. **Entry**: Establish the situation
2. **Development**: Advance the conflict
3. **Twist**: Create change
4. **Exit**: Leave a hook

### Sensory Details
- Sight, sound, smell, touch, taste
- Concrete details are more powerful than abstract descriptions
- Choose meaningful details

## Dialogue Writing

### Principles
- Each character has a unique voice
- Dialogue should advance plot or reveal character
- Avoid information dumping
- Use subtext appropriately

### Techniques
- Interrupt dialogue with action
- Cut pleasantries
- Show don't tell
- Conflict and tension

### Format
```
"Dialogue," character said, "continuing dialogue."

"Dialogue."

Character performed an action. "Continued speaking."
```

## Pacing Control

### Fast Pacing
- Short sentences
- Action scenes
- Tense atmosphere
- Reduce description

### Slow Pacing
- Long sentences
- Inner monologue
- Detailed description
- Reflective moments

### Pacing Variation
- Buffer needed after climaxes
- Balance tension and release to maintain reader interest
- Adjust based on plot needs

## Suspense and Foreshadowing

### Types of Suspense
- **Character Suspense**: What will happen to the character?
- **Plot Suspense**: What happens next?
- **Mystery Suspense**: What's the truth?

### Foreshadowing Techniques
- Plant details in advance
- Neither too obvious nor too hidden
- Pay off later in the story
- Create "aha" moments

## Emotional Rendering

### Techniques
- **Show Don't Tell**: Express emotions through action and detail
- **Contrast Enhancement**: Contrast joy with sadness
- **Pacing Control**: Emotions need buildup
- **Resonance Points**: Touch on shared reader experiences

### Emotional Arc
- Emotions need to change
- Buildup before climax
- Give readers space for emotional release
