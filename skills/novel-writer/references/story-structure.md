## Three-Act Structure

The most classic story structure, suitable for most novels.

### Act 1: Setup (~25% of story)
- **Opening**: Establish the ordinary world, introduce protagonist
- **Inciting Incident**: Event that disrupts the status quo
- **First Plot Point**: Protagonist decides to embark on the journey

### Act 2: Confrontation (~50% of story)
- **Rising Action**: Series of challenges and obstacles
- **Midpoint**: Story's turning point, stakes are raised
- **Crisis**: Protagonist's darkest moment
- **Second Plot Point**: Preparation for the final confrontation

### Act 3: Resolution (~25% of story)
- **Climax**: Final confrontation
- **Falling Action**: Aftermath of the climax
- **Resolution**: Establishment of new normal

## Kishōtenketsu

Traditional East Asian narrative structure.

- **Ki (Introduction)**: Opening that establishes context and characters
- **Shō (Development)**: Plot development, layering events
- **Ten (Twist)**: Unexpected turn, surprising change
- **Ketsu (Conclusion)**: Bringing everything together, revealing theme

---

## Seven-Point Structure

A concise structure suitable for medium and long novels.

1. **Hook**: Opening hook
2. **Plot Turn 1**: Pushes story forward
3. **Pinch 1**: Adds pressure
4. **Midpoint**: Story direction changes
5. **Pinch 2**: Further escalation
6. **Plot Turn 2**: Pushes toward climax
7. **Resolution**: Final resolution
