## Physical World

### Geography
- Terrain features (mountains, rivers, plains)
- Climate characteristics
- Natural resource distribution
- Important locations (cities, ruins, secret places)

### Ecosystem
- Flora and fauna species
- Food chain structure
- Unique creatures

### World Rules
- Physical laws (different from reality?)
- Time system (calendar, day/night)
- Spatial rules (dimensions, boundaries)

## Social Systems

### Political Structure
- Form of government (monarchy, democracy, etc.)
- Power distribution
- Legal system
- International relations

### Economic System
- Currency and trade
- Production and consumption
- Class divisions
- Resource conflicts

### Cultural Elements
- Language and writing
- Religious beliefs
- Festivals and rituals
- Art forms
- Food customs

### Social Organizations
- Family structure
- Education system
- Occupational divisions
- Social mobility

## Magic/Technology Systems

### Magic System (Fantasy)
- Source of magic
- Cost of using magic
- Limitations and rules
- Different schools/systems
- Relationship with society

### Technology System (Sci-Fi)
- Technological level
- Core technology
- Social impact of technology
- Technological limitations

### Power Balance
- Who holds power?
- How is power acquired?
- What is the cost of power?

## Building Principles

1. **Internal Consistency**: Rules shouldn't contradict each other
2. **Serve the Story**: World serves the story, don't over-build
3. **Show Don't Tell**: Reveal the world through plot
4. **Leave Room**: Give readers space for imagination
