## Character Archetypes

### Classic Archetypes
- **Hero**: Story core, drives the plot
- **Mentor**: Guides the hero, provides wisdom
- **Threshold Guardian**: Tests the hero's resolve
- **Herald**: Brings news of change
- **Shapeshifter**: Unclear allegiance, adds suspense
- **Shadow**: Antagonist, represents hero's fears
- **Ally**: Supports the protagonist, provides help
- **Trickster**: Brings chaos and humor

## Character Dimensions

### Basic Information
- Name, age, appearance
- Occupation, social status
- Family background

### Internal Dimensions
- **Core Desire**: What does the character want most?
- **Core Fear**: What does the character fear most?
- **Core Lie**: The false belief the character holds
- **Core Truth**: The truth the character needs to learn

### External Dimensions
- **Public Goal**: What the character ostensibly pursues
- **Hidden Motivation**: The deeper driving force
- **Conflict Sources**: Internal and external obstacles

### Unique Identifiers
- Distinctive speech patterns
- Signature actions or habits
- Special skills or weaknesses

## Character Arcs

### Positive Arc (Growth)
Character overcomes internal flaw and becomes better.
1. Starts with a flaw
2. Faces challenges
3. Struggles and fails
4. Moment of realization
5. Victory and transformation

### Negative Arc (Fall)
Character succumbs to flaw and becomes worse.
1. Starts with hope
2. Faces temptation
3. Makes wrong choices
4. Downward spiral
5. Tragic ending

### Flat Arc (Static)
Character changes the world while remaining unchanged.
1. Starts with truth
2. Faces a world of lies
3. Maintains conviction
4. Influences others
5. Victory
